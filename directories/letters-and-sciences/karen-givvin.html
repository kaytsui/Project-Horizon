Karen Givvin

Psychology Professor

5/7/2020

1) What do you find most beautiful about the field you are in?

Studying teaching and learning means studying how individuals interact with one another or how individuals interact with materials that were created by someone else or that exist in the natural environment. I think that’s beautiful.

2) What do you think people most misunderstand about your field?

I think people have a fairly good grasp on the fact that learning is itself something you can study. Many people want to know, for instance, about how they can best prepare for an exam and they can imagine trying to answer that question scientifically. Somehow, though, I think that many people don’t think of teaching as something you can study – that there’s more than one way to approach teaching and that there are some pretty big decisions teachers make that might be informed by research. I think a common misunderstanding about my field might be a failure to recognize it as a field at all.

3) What is your favorite picture from or of your field?

I love the painting “Baby Reaching for an Apple” by Mary Cassatt. The mother is lifting the baby up and pulling the fruit closer, so that it’s within reach, but not so close that the baby doesn’t still have to reach up to grasp it. The mother could have picked the fruit herself and handed it over, but she chose not to. With the apple standing in as an idea to be taught, it illustrates how I think of good teaching.

I also really like an image in which “Teach” is reflected in “Learn.” In many languages, the two concepts use the same word and I think that’s a nice way to think about the process. This image does a good job of capturing it in English.

4) What advice do you have for undergraduate students?

Listen to a lot of music while you’re in college. For the rest of your life, when those songs are played, they’ll bring you back to your college experience.
